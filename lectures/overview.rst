=========
Overview
=========

.. toctree::
   :maxdepth: 1

   introduction/notebook.ipynb

We discuss the basic motivation behind structural econometric modeling.
We quickly focus on the class of Eckstein-Keane-Wolpin models that are often
used in labor economics to study human capital accumulation and discuss their
economic model, mathematical formulation, and alternative calibration
procedures. We explore the seminal work outlined in Keane & Wolpin (1994)
as an example.
